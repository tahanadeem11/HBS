How long does home staging typically take?

The timeline for home staging depends on the size and type of property. For vacant homes, staging typically takes 1-3 days to complete. Occupied home staging may take 2-4 days as we work around your existing furniture. Partial staging of key rooms can often be completed in a single day. We'll provide a detailed timeline during your initial consultation based on your specific property needs.

What's included in a home staging consultation?

Our home staging consultation includes a comprehensive property walkthrough where we assess your home's current condition, identify key areas for improvement, and provide expert recommendations. We'll discuss staging strategies, furniture placement, decor suggestions, and provide a detailed action plan. You'll receive professional advice on maximizing your property's appeal, pricing considerations, and timeline recommendations tailored to the Pleasanton CA market.

Do I need to move out during staging?

No, you don't need to move out for occupied home staging. We specialize in staging homes while you're still living in them. Our team works strategically with your existing furniture and belongings, making adjustments and additions as needed. For vacant home staging, the property should be empty, but we handle all furniture and decor placement. We'll coordinate with you to minimize disruption during the staging process.

How much does home staging cost?

Home staging costs vary based on the size of your property, type of staging (vacant, occupied, partial, or full), and duration needed. Factors include the number of rooms, furniture requirements, and market timeline. We offer competitive pricing tailored to the Pleasanton CA market. During your consultation, we'll provide a detailed quote based on your specific needs. Many clients find that professional staging pays for itself through faster sales and higher offers.

What's the difference between vacant and occupied staging?

Vacant home staging involves furnishing an empty property with our professional furniture, decor, and accessories to create a welcoming, lived-in feel. Occupied home staging works with your existing furniture and belongings, making strategic adjustments, decluttering, and adding professional touches to enhance your home's appeal. Both methods are effective, but vacant staging provides complete control over the aesthetic, while occupied staging is more cost-effective and less disruptive to your daily life.

Can you stage just specific rooms?

Yes, absolutely! We offer partial home staging services that focus on key rooms that have the most impact on buyers. Typically, we stage the living room, master bedroom, kitchen, and main bathroom as these are the areas buyers focus on most. Partial staging is a cost-effective option that still significantly improves your home's appeal and can help you achieve faster sales. We'll help you determine which rooms will provide the best return on investment for your property.
